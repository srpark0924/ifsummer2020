Self Assessment

My design approach to this "Bibimbop" recipe page was to arouse viewers' interest as well as make them wonder the merit of this food. Since the food itself and the images I chose already have various colors, I tried to limit the colors I used in the page, but matched up the title colors with the colors in the images for consistency.

For better understanding, I put images under the steps that need further explanation.

I designed the "tip" part differently to emphasize the necessity of making. For the people who want this food to be perfect, please do keep an eye on it!

What I like about my page

I think consistency of the colors, images and the texts work well as I payed a lot of attention to it

Emphasis is highlighted with the bold text weight and in both context-wise and design-wise, "tip" shows the empahsis.

For legibility, balance and proportion are another parts I tried to be consistent with

Overall, as my main focus was to deliver the purpose of the page, which is arousing viewers' interest and make them want to follow it, I did accomplish the purpose, but could be even better with a bit more style design by using "position".

Place I made compromises

Like I stated below in "things for change," I originally wanted to make the ingredients and instructions part to stand out a little by making the border around the div tag without the unnecessary space on the left or right. However, though I tried several things such as changing the width of them, changing the display type, or giving padding or margins, it did not show the way I wanted it to be.

Things for change

  • under 640px, maybe a little bit of space on left and right margins. (a bit tight)
  • under 450px, only span tag font size is not getting bigger, so maybe make 0.1rem bigger
  • maybe consider making the image under instructions #3 come to the center? (for the consistency when the page is shrunk)
  • maybe design-wise emphasize more on the Ingredients & Instructions?
